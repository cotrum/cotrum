work.

Hi! I'm an undergraduate researcher at RPI passionate about quantum research and particularly how it can be leveraged in the realm of cybersecurity. As a published co-author with national conference experience, I love learning about other research and sharing my own, and I am eager to explore more early quantum applications.

My current work explores the use of quantum machine learning for advanced network intrusion detection on noisy quantum hardware. See below for more info, with a journal article coming soon.

I have also researched CTF development and the ethical use of generative AI for cybersecurity training and pedagogy, which you can view more about below!


Research

Quantum Machine Learning Kernels for Network Intrusion Detection in the NISQ Era

  • This research was super fun. I got to learn all about how quantum is being researched and used for machine learning applications, particularly in cybersecurity and network intrusion detection. The project revealed that quantum kernels can be used to detect advanced network attacks without being completely corrupted by noise and error from the hardware, meaning that as quantum computers become more powerful, there is potential for quantum integration to cause speedup in current ML intrusion detection processes.

Ethics of Generative AI through Cybersecurity Training

  • Studied the ethical use of generative AI in cybersecurity awareness training by comparing training modules developed by prompt engineers, cybersecurity experts, and hybrid roles. Delivered modules to RPI students and analyzed outcomes.
  • Presented findings at national BSides St. Pete 2024 cybersecurity conference.

Augmented Reality Cybersecurity CTF Pedagogy

  • Developed AR-based CTF challenges on topics such as steganography, DNS poisoning, and MFA bypass attacks to support hands-on cybersecurity learning through an interactive AR web app.
  • Paper co-author: RC3TF, an Augmented Reality CTF: A case study in improving cybersecurity pedagogy for the undergraduate research laboratory
